Fake News Outlets

In addition to establishing the American authenticity of these fake accounts, Russian actors also sought to establish authority and power by garnering followers and posing as powerful American institutions. When looking at user popularity (in favorites vs. followers), most troll tweets fall into a dense column near the y-axis; these posts garner a lot of favorites, despite being made by users with not many followers. Many of these accounts pose as regular people, with handles like @KarenParker93, @MsM0re, and @RealRobert1987.

However, we also see a band of accounts along the x-axis that do not have very popular posts, but do have many followers- and post quite a lot. This is a striking departure from where most accounts cluster along the y-axis. Interestingly, these low-favorited, well-followed accounts are almost all fake news accounts.

Examining account handles shows just how targeted fake news accounts were. Many mentioned densely-populated American cities, like @TodayNYCity. Some even copied names of genuine news outlets but replaced the word “news” with “new,” as in @ChicagoDailyNew or @DetroitDailyNew. The high follower counts of these accounts testify to their success at impersonating news outlets.

Notice that @ChicagoDailyNew and @DetroitDailyNew have the exact same account description, aside from the location mentioned. These cookie-cutter fake news accounts provide a glimpse into the deeply systematized spread of misinformation that took place during election interference.

Legitimacy for Russian troll accounts was more than appearing American- it also meant appearing to be an authoritative source.
